NM2207 Final Project Assignment
"Catch Puggy the Pug" Game
Zhang Yinxingzi (A0116000J)


In this final project, I’ve created a simple game. I got my inspiration from the game called ‘whack a mole’. In my game, in a specific timeframe, users will be “clicking” on the object (the pug) to “hit” or in this case “catch” the pug that appears randomly from different parts of the screen, and the score displayed at the end will be the total amount of times that the user has clicked on the pug.

I chose this because I could exercise most of the skills that we have learnt in lesson Week 6 (Games and Animation), Week 7 (Sounds), Week 9 (Arrays and Loops), Week 12 (Emitter).

I made the background a ballpit gif, as I thought that it would be possible to use the emitter that we have learnt in Week 12 tutorial session. The emitter shoots out a dot regularly, showing a kind of movement of balls in the ballpit. The balls emitted are set to the same size of the balls on the gif to show consistency.

I used the interval timer since I needed regular timings for the balls to be shoot out from the emitter, and it has to be consistent with the movement of balls in the background gif.

I have also created an array so as to hold the x and y position of the pug, using what we learnt from Week 9. Â Also, in the pool of ballpit, the pug is moving randomly across the 6 different positions that I’ve created.

I added a joyful music tune to the game, as the setting is in a ballpit where there’s fun going on. And I thought it was appropriate to match every click on the dog to a dog barking sound because technically in the game we are “catching” the dog by clicking it.

Everything is put onto the “SVG” page that we have been using in class, but I styled it with my own design, a pleasant light orange as the background colour, and gave it a simple font, to make it suit the aesthetic of the game, and appear aesthetically appealing to the users.
